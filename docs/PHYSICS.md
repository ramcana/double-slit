# Physics notes

## What the demo shows

Two coherent slit sources contribute complex amplitudes at each detector point.
Their path-length difference changes the relative phase, producing constructive
and destructive interference. A finite slit width supplies the broader
single-slit diffraction envelope.

For small angles in the far field, bright-fringe spacing is approximately

```text
Δy = λL/d
```

where `λ` is wavelength, `L` is screen distance, and `d` is slit-centre
separation. Increasing wavelength or screen distance spreads fringes apart;
increasing slit separation brings them closer together. Increasing slit width
narrows the diffraction envelope.

## Assumptions

- monochromatic, mutually coherent sources;
- scalar waves and ideal identical slits;
- arbitrary units and a fixed screen distance;
- a two-dimensional visualization of cylindrical spreading;
- a far-field finite-width envelope at the detector;
- independent random detections sampled from normalized intensity.

The colourful wave panel represents signed instantaneous amplitude, while the
detector represents non-negative time-averaged intensity. It should not be read
as a literal picture of photon trajectories.
